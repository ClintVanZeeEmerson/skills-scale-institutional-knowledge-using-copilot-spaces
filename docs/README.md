# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

OctoAcme runs projects through a lightweight but structured lifecycle: initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Teams start by defining the problem, goals, success metrics, stakeholders, and an initial risk view, then use a go/no-go decision to confirm readiness for planning. Planning turns approved initiatives into prioritized, testable backlog increments with clear acceptance criteria, dependencies, Definition of Done, and milestone/release mapping.

Delivery is built around clear role ownership across Product Managers, Project Managers, Developers, QA/testing contributors, and stakeholders. Product Managers define outcomes and priorities, Project Managers coordinate schedules, dependencies, and status, and engineering/QA roles execute implementation and validation. This role clarity supports predictable delivery and faster decision-making when trade-offs or blockers arise.

Communication and risk management are continuous, not one-time activities. Teams use regular standups, weekly delivery/status syncs, demos, and milestone updates, with a single source of truth for progress and a defined escalation path (team triage → PM/Product Lead → sponsor for high-impact issues). Risks and dependencies are tracked with owners and mitigation plans, then reviewed and updated as part of the normal delivery cadence.

Quality assurance is integrated across implementation and release. OctoAcme emphasizes unit and integration testing, end-to-end smoke checks for critical paths, CI-based linting/security scanning, manual QA when needed for acceptance, and controlled release practices including release notes, rollback planning, and post-deploy verification. Retrospectives after sprints, releases, and incidents convert lessons learned into owned action items for continuous improvement.
